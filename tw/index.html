Content Management Research Project

My Role: Project Lead

One of the greatest challenges in the the clientel web industry is the issue of content management. How will you update a site once its built? Will it require a technical user? Or, will you implement a system which allows anyone to make content updates? These are questions which can completely alter the blueprint of a website.

Troy Web often builds sites that require a CMS. To help standardize Troy Webs process for CMS implementation, I led a research project to test the viability of various plugins, apps and systems. The goal of the project was to create a comprehensive breakdown of all possible CMSs and convey the strengths, weaknesses and usability of each.

To test the viability of each CMS, I documented my experience while creating a multitude of mock websites. I also spent time analyzing the documentation of each CMS, while cross referencing my findings with other user cases.
